## Don't Ignore These Brake Warning Signs

Your brakes are the most important safety system on your vehicle. Here are five signs it's time to bring your car in for brake service.

### 1. Squealing or Squeaking

A high-pitched squeal when braking usually means your brake pads have worn down to the wear indicator.

### 2. Grinding Noise

If squealing turns to grinding, you've likely worn through the pads entirely. **Don't drive on grinding brakes.**

### 3. Vibration When Braking

A pulsing brake pedal often indicates warped rotors — common after heavy braking on Altamont Pass and I-580.

### 4. Soft or Spongy Pedal

If your brake pedal sinks to the floor, you may have a fluid leak. **This is a safety emergency.**

### 5. Pulling to One Side

Pulling when braking indicates uneven pad wear or a stuck caliper.
